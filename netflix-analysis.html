[01] Overview

Executive Summary

Netflix's catalogue has grown rapidly over the past decade, with the strongest expansion occurring after 2015 as the company increased investment in original programming and international content.

This analysis found that the United States remains Netflix's largest source of content, while countries such as India, South Korea, Japan and the United Kingdom continue to strengthen the platform's global catalogue.

Movies represent approximately 69% of all titles, indicating that feature-length productions remain Netflix's primary offering. Audience ratings also reveal a strong emphasis on mature content, suggesting that Netflix primarily targets teenage and adult viewers.

To support these findings, an interactive Power BI dashboard was developed, enabling stakeholders to explore trends dynamically through filters for countries, genres, ratings and content types.

[08] Detailed Evidence

Project Catalog Findings

FINDING 01

Netflix's rapid expansion accelerated after 2015.

What we observed

Annual releases increased dramatically, reaching 1,121 titles in 2018.

Why it matters

This reflects Netflix's strategic shift toward original programming and international expansion during one of the company's fastest growth periods.

Business Impact

Executives can use this trend to evaluate how historical investment strategies influenced catalogue growth and support future content planning.

FINDING 02

The United States remains Netflix's largest source of content.

What we observed

The United States contributes the largest absolute share of production volume, while key regions like India, South Korea, Japan and the United Kingdom continue to strengthen the platform's global catalogue.

Why it matters

While geographic localization continues expanding, a significant volume of portfolio dependency anchors inside mature domestic infrastructure networks.

Business Impact

Optimizes content spend alignment between core domestic audiences and growing international subscriber bases.

FINDING 03

Movies dominate the catalog profile share over TV Shows.

What we observed

Movies represent approximately 69% of all titles, indicating that feature-length productions remain Netflix's primary offering.

Why it matters

Feature-length titles are highly efficient for new viewer acquisitions, while episodic structural designs build long-term retention metrics.

Business Impact

Directs appropriate asset mix strategies when reviewing content investments and licensing distribution contracts.

FINDING 04

Target classifications lean heavily toward mature audiences.

What we observed

Audience ratings reveal a strong emphasis on mature content, suggesting that Netflix primarily targets teenage and adult viewers.

Why it matters

Content investments mirror the primary paying demographic brackets of single households and older audience streams.

Business Impact

Enables marketing analytics divisions to tailor promotional content toward high-yielding adult viewer accounts.

[09] Business Strategy

Strategic Recommendations

1. Expand International Hub Production: Balance structural catalog dependencies by funding independent localized content networks in expanding high-growth territories.

2. Maximize Content Mix Efficiencies: Optimize film acquisition budgets to anchor upfront user retention while designing targeted serialized episodic streams to improve subscriber lifetime value.
